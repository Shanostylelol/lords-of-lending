# Lords of Lending — LinkedIn Content Strategy

## The Play

You have 43 articles, 20 podcast episodes, 3 tools, 15 industry guides, and 50 state guides. That's not a content library — that's a 6-month LinkedIn machine. Every post drives to one of two places: the free article (builds trust) or learn.lordsoflending.com (converts).

**Primary CTA:** learn.lordsoflending.com/pricing (The Broker's Codex)
**Secondary CTA:** Free article on lordsoflending.com (top-of-funnel)

---

## Target Audiences (in priority order)

1. **Aspiring SBA Originators** — People who want to break into SBA brokering. They search "how to become an SBA loan broker." They're your buyers.
2. **Bank BDOs exploring independence** — Sitting at a bank, producing deals, wondering if they can go solo. Your originator track speaks directly to them.
3. **Commercial loan brokers** — Already brokering conventional, want to add SBA. They need training on SBA-specific packaging.
4. **Business buyers / acquisition entrepreneurs** — Searching for SBA info. They're borrower-side but some convert to originators.
5. **CPA/attorneys advising business owners** — Referral partners. They share your content with clients.

## The Funnel (Every Post)

```
Hook (stop the scroll — 2 lines max)
  ↓
Story/Value (3-5 lines of insight they can USE)
  ↓
Proof (stat, deal example, or credential)
  ↓
CTA (soft: "Full breakdown →" link OR hard: "This is Module 3 of The Broker's Codex")
```

**Rule: Never say "check out our article." Say what's IN it that they need.**

## Post Format Rules

1. **First line is everything.** If they don't stop scrolling, nothing else matters.
2. **No emojis in hooks.** Emojis scream "marketing person wrote this."
3. **Write like you talk.** Shane's voice: blunt, fast, metaphors. Not corporate.
4. **Numbers beat adjectives.** "$1.2M restaurant deal, 45 days" beats "great opportunity."
5. **End every post with ONE link.** LinkedIn buries posts with links, so put the link in the first comment OR use "link in comments."
6. **Tag people sparingly.** Only tag if they're mentioned or would genuinely engage.
7. **Hashtags: max 3.** #SBALoans #SmallBusinessLending #SBABroker

## Graphics Strategy

For each post, create one of these visual types:

### Type A: "Stat Card" (most shareable)
- Dark background (#231F20)
- One big number or stat in gold (#E2A970)
- One line of context in white
- Lords of Lending logo bottom-right
- Example: "$450M+" / "in SBA deals funded" / lordsoflending.com

### Type B: "Quote Card" (authority builder)
- Dark background
- Shane/Steph/Brian headshot (small, top-left)
- Quote in white, key phrase in gold
- Name + title below
- Example: "Most SBA deals don't die from bad credit. They die from bad packaging." — Shane Pierson

### Type C: "Checklist/List Card" (save-worthy)
- Dark background
- "5 Things Lenders Check First" or "SBA Deal Killers"
- Numbered list with gold numbers
- "Full guide → lordsoflending.com" at bottom
- These get saved and shared more than any other format

### Type D: "Before/After" or "This vs. That"
- Split card design
- Left side (red-tinted): Wrong approach
- Right side (gold-tinted): Right approach
- Example: "How most brokers package" vs. "How deals actually close"

### Type E: "Podcast Audiogram" (engagement driver)
- 30-60 second video clip from podcast
- Waveform animation overlay
- Captions burned in
- Episode title + guest name

## Carousel Strategy (LinkedIn loves these)

Create 5-8 slide carousel PDFs for pillar articles:

1. **"How to Become an SBA Broker in 2026"** — 7 slides: hook, 5 steps, CTA
2. **"SBA 7(a) vs 504: Which One?"** — 6 slides: comparison table, use cases, CTA
3. **"Why SBA Deals Fall Apart"** — 6 slides: top 5 reasons, how to fix each, CTA
4. **"SBA Fee Structures Explained"** — 8 slides: fee breakdown visuals, CTA
5. **"Build a $10M Pipeline in 12 Months"** — 7 slides: quarterly breakdown, CTA

Carousels get 2-3x the engagement of single images on LinkedIn.

## DM Playbook (Converting Engagement to Enrollments)

When someone comments, likes, or shares an originator-focused post:

**Day 0 (within 2 hours):** Reply to their comment publicly. Add value, not a pitch.

**Day 1:** Send DM:
> "Hey [name] — saw your comment on [topic]. Are you actively brokering SBA deals or looking to get into it?"

**Day 2-3 (if they respond):** Share a specific article relevant to their situation:
> "We put together a deep dive on exactly that — [link]. Curious what you think."

**Day 5-7 (if they engaged with article):** Soft pitch:
> "This is actually the foundation of what we teach in The Broker's Codex — our full SBA originator training. If you're serious about building a pipeline, it might be worth a look: learn.lordsoflending.com/pricing"

**Never pitch in the first message. Always lead with value.**
