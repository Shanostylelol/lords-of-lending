## Executive Summary

The SBA lending education space is **fragmented and underserved**. No single competitor combines all three pillars that Lords of Lending can own:

1. **Media Channel** (podcast + YouTube + blog)
2. **Education Platform** (structured courses for originators)
3. **Tools** (calculators, checklists, templates)

The closest competitors each dominate one pillar but leave the others weak:
- **Coleman Report** = Newsletter/data king, but dated UX and $995+ paywalls
- **Art of SBA Lending (Ray Drew)** = Podcast king (170+ episodes), but light on written SEO content and tools
- **NAGGL** = Association/certification authority, but members-only and institutional
- **SBA Expert Course** = Best structured course, but no media engine driving organic traffic

**Lords of Lending's opportunity:** Be the modern, media-first platform that combines all three -- the "NerdWallet of SBA originator education."

## Content Gaps to Exploit

### Gap 1: Originator-Focused Content (MASSIVE)
Nobody creates content specifically for SBA loan originators/brokers. Coleman targets underwriters and bank staff. Ray Drew targets BDOs (employed at lenders). Credit Suite targets broad "make money" seekers. **No one creates content for the independent originator who sources SBA deals.** This is LoL's primary gap to own.

### Gap 2: Free SBA Education (HIGH VALUE)
Coleman's best content is paywalled at $995+/year. NAGGL requires membership. SBA.gov is bureaucratic. There is no "free, high-quality, comprehensive SBA education hub" on the internet. LoL can be that hub (with learn.lordsoflending.com as the premium upgrade).

### Gap 3: SBA Lending News (WEEKLY)
Coleman has a daily email but it's paywalled. Nobody publishes free, weekly SBA lending news roundups. An "SBA Lending This Week" blog post series would capture huge recurring traffic and be perfect for newsletter signups.

### Gap 4: Industry-Specific SBA Guides
Very few deep-dive guides exist for specific industries:
- SBA loans for restaurants (LoL has an episode -- turn into a 3,000+ word guide)
- SBA loans for hotels/motels
- SBA loans for gas stations/C-stores
- SBA loans for franchises (by franchise brand)
- SBA loans for medical/dental practices
- SBA loans for auto repair/service businesses

### Gap 5: SBA Loan Calculators & Tools
No competitor offers a comprehensive calculator suite:
- SBA 7(a) payment calculator (with SBA guarantee fee built in)
- SBA loan eligibility pre-screener
- Debt service coverage ratio calculator
- SBA equity injection calculator
- SBA loan comparison tool (7a vs 504 vs Express)

### Gap 6: Video Content on YouTube
Ray Drew has some YouTube presence but it's minimal. No one is systematically creating YouTube content for SBA lending education. Short-form (YouTube Shorts, TikTok) for SBA tips is completely untapped.

### Gap 7: AI/Tech in SBA Lending
Coleman has an "AI SBA Underwriter" membership but no one is creating content about how AI and technology are changing SBA lending. This is a trending topic with low competition.
