## Why Equity (Not Cash Flow) Makes or Breaks SBA Deals | Episode 20

The Lords bring in the OG of OGs—Lance Sexton, former SBA deputy director who ran liquidations for years. This episode flips the conventional wisdom: it's not cash flow that kills SBA deals. It's equity.

## Meet the Guest: Lance Sexton

Lance has spent his career on the backend of SBA lending—the part nobody wants to talk about. When deals go bad, he's the cleanup man. He's seen firsthand what happens when borrowers default on thin deals, and his perspective is unlike anything you'll hear on other podcasts.

## Key Takeaways

### Equity Is the #1 Deal Killer

Steph changed her tune: "I used to think cash flow was the most important thing. I have changed my song now. Equity is the number one deal killer. If you don't put in enough, you don't have enough, it will kill your deal."

### The Liquidation Perspective

Lance drops a truth bomb most sales teams never hear: "If a seller finances 50% of a deal and only 5% of it's equity injection, I fall in love with that loan because that's gonna be a really easy loan to liquidate." When borrowers have minimal equity, they see their bankruptcy attorney the next day after default.

### Every Sales Guy Should See a Liquidation

Shane's take: "Every sales guy would wear a different hat if they went through liquidation. To understand how ugly it gets." The disconnect between origination and what happens when deals fail is massive—and it's costing the industry.

## What This Means for Originators

Stop chasing volume and start chasing quality. A thin deal that closes is a ticking time bomb. Understanding the liquidation side of SBA lending makes you a better originator, a better advisor to your borrowers, and ultimately more profitable.

## What This Means for Business Buyers

If you're buying a business, bring real cash to the table. Seller notes with minimal equity injection aren't the creative win you think they are—they're a liability. The best deals are the ones where the buyer has genuine skin in the game.
