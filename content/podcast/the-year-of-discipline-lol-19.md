## The Year of Discipline | Episode 19

2025 was the year of volume. 2026 is the year of discipline. In this episode, the Lords tackle one of the most critical—and most ignored—topics in SBA lending: equity injection.

## Key Takeaways

### Ownership Isn't Deserved—It's Earned

For years, access to capital expanded, credit boxes stretched, and deals got thinner and thinner. The name of the game was 100% financing or buying a business with as little cash as possible. That era is ending. The SBA pumped out $100 billion last year, and corrections are coming.

### The Black Card Analogy

Shane breaks it down: 2025 was like the friend at dinner who says "just put it on the black card"—unlimited, no questions asked. 2026 is the friend who asks "how are we paying this back?" There are caps now. You have to show how the money comes back.

### What Is a Fair Equity Injection?

Brian's take: it depends on the whole picture. Cash flow, EBITDA, borrower experience, reserves, working capital runway. The borrower who can rattle off their plan gets more flexibility than the one who says "I'll just open the doors and it'll happen."

### Regulation Is Tightening—And That's a Good Thing

The Lords—all three sales professionals—admit that tightening is necessary. Banks are hurting, over-leveraging is real, and the SBA program can't sustain deals with zero skin in the game.

## What This Means for You

If you're looking to buy a business in 2026, come prepared with real equity. The days of creative workarounds with minimal cash are numbered. Lenders are getting back to basics, and that means borrowers who show up with skin in the game will close faster and on better terms.
